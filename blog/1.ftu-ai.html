FTU Students in AI Olympiad 2025

2025-12-20 · FTU Labs · 4 min read

We are proud to share that students from the School of Technology and Data Science (Khoa Công nghệ và Khoa học Dữ liệu) at Foreign Trade University achieved outstanding results at three major competitions in 2025: the Olympic Tin học (Informatics Olympiad), the Olympic AI, and the ICPC (International Collegiate Programming Contest).

Olympic AI 2025

2025 marked the first year that the Vietnam Informatics Association organized the Olympic AI competition. The FTU Olympic AI team, coached by Hau Tran, Khanh Pham, Viet Nguyen, and Minh Tran from the Department of Computer Science (Khoa Công nghệ và Khoa học Dữ liệu), achieved:

  • Third Prize (Giải Ba) — Northern Regional Round
  • Encouragement Prize (Giải Khuyến khích) — National Round

Team members: Hiep Nguyen, Huyen Nguyen, My Nguyen.

Foreign Trade University was the only economics-focused university to win a prize at the Olympic AI 2025 — a strong statement about FTU’s growing capabilities in technology and artificial intelligence.

Olympic Tin học 2025

Non-specialized Track (Bảng Không chuyên Tin)

  • Second Prize (Giải Nhì) — Nguyễn Đức Hiệp, Võ Minh Ngọc
  • Encouragement Prize — Nguyễn Khánh Huyền

Specialized Track (Bảng Chuyên Tin)

  • Third Prize (Giải Ba) — Trần Hương Ly
  • Encouragement Prize — Đặng Vũ Quỳnh Trang, Đinh Bảo Ngọc

ICPC 2025

The FTU Knights 2 team won Second Prize in the Non-specialized Division at the ICPC 2025, continuing to demonstrate the competitive programming strength of FTU students on the international stage.

What This Means for FTU

These results are a clear testament to the academic spirit, innovation, and technological integration at Foreign Trade University. While FTU is traditionally known for economics and international trade, our students are proving that they can compete at the highest level in computer science and AI — standing shoulder to shoulder with peers from dedicated engineering and technology schools.

Congratulations to all the coaches, the Department of Computer Science, the Department of Informatics, and every student who worked hard to achieve these remarkable results.
